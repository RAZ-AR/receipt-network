# UX Principles

## 1. Value before explanation

The first interaction should demonstrate value before asking the user to understand the ecosystem.

## 2. Scan first

The primary action is always easy to find and may be used before a long onboarding flow.

## 3. Immediate feedback

Every receipt action must produce a visible state: uploaded, processing, verified, rejected, or needs attention.

## 4. Three motivation horizons

Home shows no more than three major goals:

1. reward progress;
2. lottery progress;
3. opportunity of the day.

## 5. Trust over excitement

Points, tickets, rules, rejection reasons and draw timing must be understandable and auditable.

## 6. Recovery is part of the flow

Poor image quality, duplicate receipts, OCR uncertainty and unavailable rewards require clear recovery actions.

## 7. One primary action per screen

Secondary actions must not compete with the main job of the screen.

## 8. Progressive disclosure

Advanced mechanics are shown only when they become relevant.

## 9. No dark patterns

Notifications, reviews and partner promotions must be transparent and easy to control.

## 10. Accessible by default

The experience should support readable contrast, scalable text, simple language and screen-reader labels.
