# Design Foundations

## Design Goal

Make additional value visible immediately, without making the product feel like a casino, a banking app or a coupon catalogue.

## Experience Character

The interface should feel:

- trustworthy;
- simple;
- optimistic;
- rewarding;
- practical;
- locally relevant.

It should not feel:

- noisy;
- manipulative;
- overly luxurious;
- childish;
- financially speculative.

## Visual Hierarchy

1. Current value and progress.
2. Primary scan action.
3. Three motivation horizons.
4. Secondary history and discovery.

## Layout

- mobile-first;
- one primary action per view;
- cards represent independent value objects;
- critical numbers are large and paired with labels;
- avoid dense dashboards for consumers;
- business and admin surfaces may use tables and denser layouts.

## Spacing System

Use a base unit of 4 with a practical scale:

- 4: micro spacing;
- 8: related elements;
- 12: compact card internals;
- 16: default spacing;
- 24: section separation;
- 32: major separation;
- 48: hero spacing.

## Shape

- cards: medium radius;
- buttons: clear, comfortable radius;
- status chips: pill shape;
- receipt frames: subtle corners, not playful;
- avoid excessive rounded containers nested inside one another.

## Color Roles

Exact colors remain open. Required semantic roles:

- brand primary;
- background;
- surface;
- text primary;
- text secondary;
- success;
- warning;
- error;
- information;
- points/value accent;
- lottery accent;
- partner accent.

Points and lottery should have distinct identities, because guaranteed value and chance are different mechanics.

## Motion

Motion communicates state changes:

- receipt captured;
- receipt verified;
- points added;
- goal progress changed;
- ticket issued;
- reward unlocked.

Avoid casino-style spinning, flashing or artificial near-win effects.

## Content Style

- lead with the result;
- use concrete numbers;
- explain rejection reasons plainly;
- avoid terms such as ecosystem, gamification and digital asset in consumer copy;
- always show the recovery action after an error.

## Trust Signals

- verified receipt marker;
- visible rules for points and draws;
- draw history;
- clear expiration dates;
- reason for every rejection;
- distinction between pending and confirmed value;
- disclosure of sponsored opportunities.
